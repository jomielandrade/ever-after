# Design System

**Project:** Project Ever After
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Active

---

# Purpose

This document defines the visual identity and interaction principles of Project Ever After.

Its purpose is to ensure every screen, component, and animation contributes to a cohesive luxury editorial experience.

---

# Brand Personality

Project Ever After should feel:

* Quiet Luxury
* Editorial
* Romantic
* Warm
* Timeless
* Elegant
* Minimal
* Handcrafted

It should never feel flashy, trendy, or overly decorative.

---

# Brand Keywords

Always optimize for:

* Simplicity
* Spaciousness
* Refinement
* Warmth
* Emotion
* Craftsmanship

Avoid:

* Clutter
* Heavy gradients
* Neon colors
* Cartoon illustrations
* Excessive floral graphics
* Overly playful interactions

Small

Medium

16px

---

# Shadows

Use soft shadows only.

Avoid harsh drop shadows.

The interface should feel layered through spacing rather than depth.

---

# Buttons

Primary

* Dusty Blue background
* White text
* Rounded corners
* Subtle hover elevation

Secondary

* Transparent background
* Dusty Blue border
* Dusty Blue text

Text

* Minimal underline or opacity transition

---

# Cards

Cards should:

* Have generous padding
* Rounded corners
* Soft borders
* Minimal shadow
* Spacious layouts

---

# Icons

Style:

* Thin line icons
* Elegant
* Minimal

Avoid filled icon sets.

---

# Photography

Images should appear:

* Bright
* Warm
* Natural
* Authentic

Avoid:

* Heavy filters
* Oversaturation
* Busy compositions

Large editorial photography should be prioritized.

---

# Motion Principles

Motion should feel:

* Gentle
* Slow
* Elegant
* Purposeful

Preferred transitions:

* Fade
* Crossfade
* Soft slide
* Subtle scale

Avoid:

* Bounce
* Shake
* Elastic
* Spin
* Flash

Respect the user's reduced-motion preference.

---

# Component Guidelines

Every component should:

* Have one clear purpose.
* Be reusable.
* Be responsive.
* Be accessible.
* Minimize visual noise.

---

# Accessibility

Maintain:

* WCAG AA contrast where practical
* Keyboard navigation
* Visible focus states
* Semantic HTML

Accessibility is part of the design language.

---

# Responsive Breakpoints

Primary focus:

* Mobile (default)
* Tablet
* Desktop

Design mobile-first and progressively enhance larger screens.

---

# Inspiration

The visual language should take inspiration from:

* Apple product pages
* Editorial magazines
* Luxury wedding stationery
* Handmade cotton paper
* Embossed invitations
* Fine art photography

The final experience should feel original rather than directly imitating any single source.

---

## Motion Tokens

### Durations

- Fast — 0.2s
- Standard — 0.4s
- Elegant — 0.6s
- Slow — 0.8s

### Distances

- Small — 8px
- Medium — 16px
- Large — 24px

### Principles

- Motion should guide attention.
- Motion should never delay interaction.
- Motion should respect prefers-reduced-motion.
- Favor opacity and gentle movement.
- Avoid bounce, elastic effects, and excessive rotation.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

The design system is complete when:

* All components use the defined color palette.
* Typography remains consistent.
* Spacing follows the 8-point grid.
* Motion aligns with the documented principles.
* Accessibility standards are maintained.
* The overall experience communicates quiet luxury and timeless elegance.
